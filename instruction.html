Running the Shiny App:

Abbreviations:

SS  — Sample Size
TRT  — Treatment
PBO  — Placebo
ET  — Early Termination
EFU  — Efficacy Follow-Up

1. Set a random seed.

2. Enter the number of simulations.

3. Enter the two-sided α in the box labeled Significant Level.

4. Enter the total SS for each trial data (total number of subjects planned to be randomized) in the box labeled Sample Size.

5. For TRT Rate, enter 0.5 if the TRT-to-PBO ratio is 1:1; enter 0.67 if the TRT-to-PBO ratio is 2:1.

6. Choose the number of post-baseline visits.

7. Enter the baseline mean, common standard deviation, and correlation among visits.

8. For each of the post-baseline visits:

    (1) Efficacy is the expected difference between TRT and PBO for subjects who have stayed on study treatment until that visit.

    (2) EFU Efficacy is the expected difference between TRT and PBO for the ET subjects who have been assessed for efficacy at the EFU visit.

    (3) EFU Rate is the proportion of ET subjects assessed for efficacy at the EFU visit.

9. Specify the ET Rate for each arm at each post-baseline visit. For example, if 15% of TRT subjects and 12% of PBO subjects discontinue study treatment at the end of post-baseline visit 1 and an additional (based on the original enrollment) 10% of TRT subjects and 9% of PBO subjects discontinue study treatment at the end of post-baseline visit 2 (as shown in the table below), enter 0.15 for TRT ET Rate 1, 0.09 for PBO ET Rate 1, 0.1 for TRT ET Rate 2, and 0.06 for PBO ET Rate 2.

Baseline SS Post-Baseline 1 SS Post-Baseline 2 SS Cumulative ET Rate
TRT 100 100 - 100*15% = 85 85 - 100*10% = 75 25%
PBO 100 100 - 100*9% = 91 91 - 100*6% = 85 15%
Overall 200 176 160 20%

10. Click the Simulate button to generate trial data based on the input and initiate the power calculation (proportion of times the null hypothesis is rejected) for the simulation trials.

11. You can browse the first trial of simulated data and download the entire set of simulated trial data in an Excel sheet.
