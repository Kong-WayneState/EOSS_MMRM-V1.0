Running the Shiny App

Abbreviations: TRT -- treatment; EFU -- efficacy follow-up; ET -- early termination (from study treatment); PBO -- placebo; SS -- sample size.

1. Set a random seed.

2. Enter the number of simulations.

3. Enter the two-sided alpha in the box labeled Significant Level.

4. Enter the total sample size of the trial (total number of subjects planned to be randomized) in the box labeled Sample Size.

5. For TRT Rate, enter 0.5 if treatment:placebo = 1:1; enter 0.67 if treatment: placebo = 2:1.

6. Choose the number of post baseline visits.

7. Enter the baseline mean, common standard deviation and correlation among visits.

8. For each of the post baseline visits:

    (1) Efficacy is the expected difference between treatment and placebo for subjects who have stayed on study treatment until that visit.

    (2) EFU Efficacy is the expected difference between treatment and placebo for the ET subjects who have been assessed for efficacy at the EFU visit.

    (3) EFU Rate is the proportion of the ET subjects who have been assessed for efficacy at the EFU visit.

9. Specify the ET Rate for each arm at each post baseline visit. For example, if 10% TRT subjects and 5% PBO subjects discontinue study treatment at the end of post baseline visit 1 and an additional 15% TRT subjects and 2% PBO subjects discontinue study treatment at the end of post baseline visit 2 (as shown in the table below), enter 0.1 for ET Rate TRT 1, 0.05 for ET Rate PBO 1, 0.15 for ET Rate TRT 2, and 0.02 for ET Rate PBO 2.

Baseline SS Post Baseline 1 SS Post Baseline 2 SS Cumulative ET Rate
TRT 100 100 - 100*10% = 90 90 - 100*15% = 75 25%
PBO 100 100 - 100*5% = 95 95 - 100*2% = 93 7%
Overall 200 185 168 16%

10. Click the Simulate button to simulate trial power.

11. You can browse the first trial of simulated data and download the entire simulated trial data in an Excel sheet.
